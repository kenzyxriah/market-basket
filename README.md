# market-basket

Project Scenario
You have been hired as the Lead Data Analyst for a UK-based non-store online
retail company. The executive team has historically relied on static, flat-file
reports to view total sales. They now want to transition to prescriptive analytics.

Stakeholders are asking critical questions: Who are our most valuable customers
versus those we are about to lose? What are our actual seasonal revenue trends
when we factor out returns? Most importantly, how can we strategically bundle
products to increase our Average Order Value (AOV)?

Your objective is to ingest over 500,000 rows of messy, historical transactional
data and build a robust, interactive Business Intelligence tool. This tool must
clean the data, establish a relational data architecture, segment the customer
base, and serve as a dynamic recommendation engine for the marketing team.
